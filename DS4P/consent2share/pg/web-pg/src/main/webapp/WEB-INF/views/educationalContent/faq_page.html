Why should I share my information?

Sharing your medical information can help improve your health by:

  • Connecting all of your providers
  • Coordinating your health care needs
  • Helping you to receive better care

Back to top

Do I have to sign up right away?

No. You can sign up whenever you are ready. For security and privacy purposes, you will need assistance from a staff member to set up your C2S account. Once your account is set up, you can take your time registering with C2S and creating your consent policy.

Back to top

What happens if I forget my C2S user name or password?

If you forget your user name or password, you can reset it by going to the C2S Website, enter the email address on file for you in the C2S system, and then follow the reset instructions that are sent to your email address. You can also change your user name or password at any time.

Back to top

How long is my consent good for?

  • You determine how long your consent is good for.
  • HIPAA law restricts the length of consents to no more than 1 year, and the C2S system adheres to the HIPAA law.
  • You can revoke or cancel your consent at any time in just a few steps.

Back to top

What information is shared?

  • All of your historical medical information can be shared when you consent.
  • C2S shares information like your diagnosis, medical history, lab tests, medications and allergies.
  • C2S does not share the content of conversations with your provider, or progress notes from your provider or therapist.

Back to top

How will I know what information is being shared about me?

When you create your consent policy, you decide what information you want shared with your providers. For details about the information you consent to share, you can use the "Try my policy" feature in C2S to view what you have selected.

Back to top

Will I be notified by C2S whenever my information is shared with my providers?

C2S does not automatically notify you when your information is shared. Please talk to your doctor or provider about the information you consent to share.
